FAQs for Assignment1

 

1.    What is the theme about? What are we expected to model?

A:    The theme has been chosen because it is very open. You are free to choose anything you want to draw, so long as it is somehow related to the theme.  

2.    Are we allowed to use any third-party modeller or texture generation tools?

A:    Yes. As you should have seen by now, POV-Ray is very much an end-user software. You do not exactly know how the scene is actually generated. You just define all the components. Therefore, I'd like to encourage everyone to explore as much as possible all available tools and use them to generate your final product. However, all final images must be generated in one pass.

        During the assessment, you are expected to tell the tutor what external tools you have used, where they are from, and demonstrate your ability to change some aspect of them. 

3.    What coordinate system are we using? Left-handed or right-handed?

A:    In lecture, we mentioned that the convention for CG 200 (252/502) is the right-handed coordinate system. However, left-handed coordinate system is used in POV-Ray. So for Assignment 1, you are to use left-hand coordinate system, since that's the system default. As a matter of fact, there is no "right or wrong" in this kind of choice, such as left-handed or right-handed, row representation or column representation, etc. You just have to consciously ensure the consistency of them when you are building a system. Otherwise undesired results will be generated (and sometimes hard to be detected) since ultimately all transformations appear as matrix multiplications.

4.    What is the default size of the final image?

A:    There is no default size for the final image. You are free to choose any size which fits your design.

5.    Are we expected to produce a smooth stream of animation? How?

A:    Yes. You are expected to pre-render your complicated final images and produce the animation beforehand. During the demonstration you will be required to make modifications on some images on spot and show them to your tutor. 

    Please follow the following steps to convert you images to a smooth animation series:

           1.  Convert your frames into animated gif. The syntax is:

            %convert <file*>.<png/jpg/…> <file>.gif

    You can use option –delay to adjust the frame rate. Eg.,

            %convert –delay 100 assign1*.png Assign1.gif

2.  Use “gthumb” to display the animated gif. Eg.

            %gthumb Assign1.gif

 

6.    How is the assignment assessed? Does it depend on whether the tutor likes the image?

A:    The marking of the assessment will be based on the technical aspect shown in your final image, such as the number of objects, the complexity of the objects and the proper choice of other features, such as textures or transparency etc.  It is very difficult to judge the aesthetic aspect of an image, and we do not intend to use that for our marking. However, I do plan to have another on-line exhibition of the "beautiful" images. If your image is chosen to appear in the exhibition it simply means that we like it. It does not necessarily reflect in the final marking, although most of the time the images in the exhibition do earn very high marks for their creators.  

          The Marking Guide for this assignment is also published for your reference.

7.    What is the final submission? Are we expected to keep all 30 image files in our directory?

A:    Please leave only 1 final image and 1 animation sequence in your directory. Actually the tutor's assessment during the demonstration is the final assessment for this assignment. Please leave you images in you directory so that we can get it if it is required for whatever purpose later.
